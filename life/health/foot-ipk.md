## Root Cause Analysis

### 1. Biomechanical (Primary Suspect)

**Foot Type:** Pes Cavus (high arch) with rigid supination
- Foot fails to pronate → locked outward
- 5th metatarsal becomes a "battering ram" with every step
- Focal pressure → keratin packs into hard core (nucleus)

**The Big Toe Problem:**
- Big toe hiding under 2nd toe (Hallux adduction)
- "Windlass Mechanism" broken — big toe can't anchor
- 100% forefoot load shifts to lateral (pinky) side
- **Biomechanical trap:** Can't escape the outer rail

**Weak Link:** Peroneal muscles (Longus & Brevis)
- Should evert foot (lift pinky side, plant big toe side)
- Likely weak/inhibited → foot defaults to supination

### 2. Cellular (If standard treatment keeps failing)

**Possible:** Punctate Porokeratosis
- NOT a friction callus — genetic/clonal keratinocyte mutation
- Mevalonate kinase pathway defect
- Forms "Cornoid Lamella" — shaving triggers faster regrowth (Koebner phenomenon)
- Would explain why surgery fails and it keeps returning

**Diagnostic clues:**
- Feels like distinct seed/plug (not diffuse thickening)
- Pain disproportionate to size (sharp, needle-like)
- Absolute resistance to orthotics/friction reduction
